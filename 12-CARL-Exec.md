The Core Structure (4 Buckets – Always in This Order)
Every behavioral or hypothetical story follows this exact sequence. Total: ~12 sentences, delivered as bullets for leadership loops.

1. Context (30–45 seconds max — directors hate setup) Lead with the punchline first (Vijay Rule + 12-Sentence #1).
	•	Hook: “I’ll tell you about the time I [led/built/drove] [specific security/analytics initiative], which delivered [X% risk reduction or $Y impact].”
	•	Role + focus areas
	•	Security/customer problem (with number)
	•	Business stakes (churn, compliance cost, revenue risk, breach exposure)
	•	Your North Star goal/metric
Vijay delivery: Say the hook, then pause. Only continue if they lean forward.

2. Action (Behavioral core — the part they judge you on) Frame as “I drove this through three main things” (Vijay’s 3-point method + 12-Sentence 6–10).
	1	Research & Key Insight (talked to stakeholders, pulled logs/data, biggest finding #1 + #2)
	2	Vision + Buy-in (the solution we chose + who I aligned, presented what artifact, got approval)
	3	Execution & Launch (built/tested with team, rollout approach, cross-functional coordination)
This keeps you concise and shows structured thinking — exactly what NVIDIA directors expect.

3. Result (Metric-first — 12-Sentence 11–12) “We moved the North Star metric from X to Y (exceeding goal by Z%), unlocking [business impact] and scaling to [next milestone or org].”

4. Learnings (Your senior differentiator — pure CARL) Acknowledge it wasn’t perfect: “One thing that didn’t go as planned was [X]. What I learned is [specific insight], so now I always [new behavior]. This directly shaped how I approach [security enablement at NVIDIA].”

Delivery Rules (Vijay’s Gold — Use These in Every Loop)
	•	Punchline First + Pause: Answer → deliberate pause → explain/educate only if they signal interest.
	•	Three-Point Discipline: For Director/VP loop → pure bullets, 45–60 seconds max.
	•	Body Language Calibration (real-time switch):
	◦	Leaning back or rapid-fire → contract to bullets only.
	◦	Leaning forward or nodding → expand with technical detail (peer mode).
	◦	Neutral → stay on framework.
	•	Prepare 3 Versions of Every Story (Vijay’s exact advice):
	◦	VP/Director: 45 seconds (bullets only)
	◦	Peer (technical TPMs): 90 seconds (more depth + collaboration)
	◦	Cross-functional (security managers): 2 minutes (heavy on influence, conflict, escalation judgment)
